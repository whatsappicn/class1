Rule 2 – Adding -es We add -es to singular nouns ending in s, ch, x, o or sh to make them plural.
class → classes  |  branch → branches  |  tax → taxes  |  hero → heroes  |  bush → bushes

Rule 3 – Nouns ending in y (consonant + y) In nouns ending in y, we change the y into i and then add -es.
story → stories  |  puppy → puppies  |  spy → spies
Rule 4 – Nouns ending in y (vowel + y) If there is a, e, i, o or u before y, we simply add -s.
day → days  |  key → keys  |  boy → boys  |  toy → toys
Rule 5 – Nouns ending in f or fe In some nouns ending in f or fe, we leave out the f/fe and add -ves.
wife → wives  |  life → lives

📘 Special Plurals – Same singular & plural
Some words change vowels to form plurals (Irregular) goose → geese  |  foot → feet  |  man → men

Some words have the SAME singular and plural form deer → deer  |  sheep → sheep  |  fish → fish

📘 Irregular Naming Words
Remember – To make a noun more than one:
  • most of the time, just add -s
  • to words ending in ch, sh, s, x or z, add -es
  • to words ending in y, drop the y and add -ies
  • to words ending in f or fe, drop the f or fe and add -ves

Irregular naming words don't follow any rules. Most naming words can be made plural by adding -en.

🐂 → 🐂🐂🐂
ox  →  oxen
👦 → 👦👦👦
child  →  children
